How it works

1

Browse your port adventures

Visit your Disney Cruise excursion page as normal. ExcursionAlert adds a "Watch Availability" button to every excursion.

2

Watch the ones you want

Click Watch on any sold-out or conflict-blocked excursion. Choose specific timeslots or watch them all.

3

Get alerted instantly

When a spot opens up, you get a browser notification and, with Cruise Pass, also get an instant push notification on your phone.

Getting started

1

Install the extension

Add ExcursionAlert to Chrome from the Chrome Web Store. No account or sign-up required.

2

Visit your Disney Cruise excursion page

Log in to disneycruise.disney.go.com and navigate to your booking's Port Adventures. ExcursionAlert automatically detects your session - you'll see your reservation number appear in the extension popup.

Important: You need to visit this page at least once each browser session for ExcursionAlert to pick up your login credentials. After that, it monitors in the background automatically.
3

Start booking an excursion to populate your party

Click Select on any excursion and proceed far enough into the booking flow that you see your party members listed - then back out without reserving the excursion. This lets ExcursionAlert learn everyone's names so you can filter alerts by who's joining each adventure.

You don't need to complete the booking. Just getting to the guest selection screen is enough. ExcursionAlert picks up the names automatically in the background.
4

Click "Watch Availability" on any excursion

ExcursionAlert adds a Watch Availability button next to the Select button on every excursion card. Click it on anything you want to monitor - sold out, conflict-blocked, or just keeping an eye on.

You can filter by specific timeslots and choose which party members the slot needs to accommodate.

5

Let it run

ExcursionAlert checks availability every 5, 10, or 15 minutes (your choice). When a spot opens up, you'll get a browser notification immediately.

Keep a browser window open with your Disney Cruise tab loaded, or check back periodically - the extension polls while the tab is active. The more browsers you have open across devices, the better your coverage.

Set up phone notifications (Cruise Pass)

With a Cruise Pass license, ExcursionAlert can send push notifications directly to your phone via ntfy - a free, open-source notification service. This means you'll get alerted even if you step away from your computer.

  1. Download the free ntfy app on your phone - available for iPhone and Android.
  2. Choose a unique topic name for yourself - something nobody else is likely to use, like smith-family-alaska-2026. You don't create an account; the topic name is the only identifier.
  3. In the ntfy app, tap + and subscribe to your topic name.
  4. In ExcursionAlert, open the popup, go to Settings, and enter your topic name in the "ntfy push to phone" field.
  5. Click Send Test to confirm notifications are arriving on your phone, then save your settings.
  6. If you use Focus modes (iPhone) or Do Not Disturb schedules (Android) that limit which apps can surface notifications, make sure to add ntfy to the allowed apps list for those modes - otherwise you may miss an alert when availability opens up.
Privacy note: ntfy topics are public by default - anyone who knows your topic name can subscribe to it. Choose something non-obvious. If you want end-to-end privacy, ntfy also supports self-hosted servers, but that's beyond the scope of most users.
